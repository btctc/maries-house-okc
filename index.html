Our Mission

Built for the Women Who Built Our Community

Marie's House Assisted Living was founded on a simple belief: every woman deserves to age in dignity, comfort, and safety — surrounded by people who truly understand her. Too many East OKC families drive across the city to find quality senior care. We're changing that.

Located at 3217 N Lindsay Ave in East Oklahoma City, Marie's House is a brand-new boutique facility serving women 65 and older. With just 20 residents, every woman is known by name. This is not a nursing home. This is a home.

  • All-women residents and all-women staff — always
  • East OKC's only modern assisted living within a 5-mile radius
  • 3 hot meals per day, personal care, activities & 24-hour supervision
  • Licensed RN clinical oversight with weekly nurse consultation
  • Medicaid ADvantage waiver beds available — 4 reserved spots

Why We Built This

Built in the Spirit of Marie

A mother. A grandmother. The heartbeat of a family — known by her middle name, Marie.

Marie was not famous. She did not need to be. She was the kind of woman who held her family together with faith, grace, and an unshakeable love for everyone who came through her door. She cooked meals that drew people in from across the neighborhood. She prayed over her children, her grandchildren, and every life she touched. She was the foundation.

Marie — the middle name of Harold Thompson's mother and Terrence Crawford's grandmother — is the name this family holds most dear. Harold's sister is Terrence's mother, making this not just a business partnership, but a family mission. When it came time for her to need more care than the family could provide alone, they faced what so many East OKC families face: there was nothing nearby that felt like home. Nothing that honored a woman like her.

"We built Marie's House because our grandmother deserved better. And so does yours."

This facility is her legacy. Every woman who walks through these doors will be cared for the way Marie deserved to be cared for — with dignity, with warmth, and by women who understand exactly how precious she is.

Our Founders

A Family Mission,
A Community Legacy

Marie's House was not built by investors looking for a return. It was built by a nephew and his uncle — carrying their family's matriarch in their hearts.

Harold Thompson

Co-Founder · OKC Managing Partner

"She gave everything to everyone around her. The least we can do is make sure women like her have a place that honors that."

Harold Thompson spent 35 years as an Oklahoma City firefighter — 35 years of running toward people in their most desperate moments. He retired as one of East OKC's most trusted and recognized public servants. But it was a personal moment that changed everything.

When his mother, Miss Marie, began to need more care than the family could provide at home, Harold faced what thousands of East OKC families face every year: there was nothing nearby that truly felt like home. Nothing that honored a woman of her stature and faith. That gap became a calling.

As OKC Managing Partner, Harold brings his fire department discipline, his 35 years of community relationships, his church network, and his deep East OKC roots to every aspect of Marie's House operations. He is on the ground, in the community, every single day.

Terrence Crawford

Co-Founder · Financing & Strategy Lead

"My grandmother was the most dignified woman I've ever known. Building Marie's House is the most important thing I'll ever do."

For Terrence Crawford, Marie's House is not a business venture — it is a tribute to the two women who made him who he is. His grandmother — Harold Thompson's mother — is known by her middle name, Marie, and was the kind of woman who held a family together through faith, grace, and unconditional love. His mother, Harold's sister, taught him that purpose matters more than profit.

When his uncle Harold came to him with the vision for Marie's House, there was never any doubt. Terrence stepped in as the financing and strategy lead, navigating the SBA loan process, building the investor structure, and managing every dollar with the discipline this mission demands.

Terrence handles all capital strategy, lender relationships, licensing, and long-term planning — so that Harold can focus on what he does best: being present in the community his mother loved.

Gwendolyn Jackson-Blade, LPN

Administrator · Director of Nursing · Activities Coordinator

An accomplished Licensed Practical Nurse with deep experience in skilled nursing and long-term care, Gwendolyn brings the clinical expertise, compassion, and steady hand that Marie's House requires. Her SNF and LTACH background means 20 residents is a lighter clinical load than her prior settings — giving her the bandwidth to be both a skilled clinician and a warm, engaged presence for residents every day.

Gwendolyn holds an active Oklahoma LPN license and is currently completing all three required RCAL administrator examinations. She will serve as the heart of daily operations — caring for residents the way she would care for her own family.

The Oklahoma ADvantage waiver is a Medicaid Home and Community Based Services program that helps income-eligible seniors pay for personal care services in an assisted living setting. Residents generally must need assistance with daily activities and have income under approximately $2,901/month (2025 limit). We have 4 beds reserved for ADvantage waiver residents. Our team can guide your family through the application process — just call or send us a message to get started.
